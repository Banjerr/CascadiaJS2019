Kafka seems like an excellent technology to build on if you're needing a real-time stream of data; it's horizontally scalable, fault-tolerant, extremely fast, and runs in production in thousands of companies. 

Imagine streaming data from an order to multiple places, instantly, with 100% certainty that the data will get there, and correctly. 

**Initially conceived as a messaging queue, Kafka is based on an abstraction of a distributed commit log. Since being created and open sourced by LinkedIn in 2011, Kafka has quickly evolved from messaging queue to a full-fledged event streaming platform.**
